Here we go…

Why I'm finally giving peptides a try

If you've known me for a while, you already know I don't jump into every new health trend that pops up. I research things until my eyeballs hurt. 😂

I've spent months reading, attending educational calls, listening to medical professionals, and watching the experiences of people I actually know. Does that prove anything? Nope. But it made me curious enough to keep learning—and finally decide it was time to begin.

The last few years took a toll. After losing my dad and spending so much time as his caregiver, I could see and feel changes in my own body. My hair became thinner through the front and middle of my scalp. My skin changed. My joints have not always been thrilled with me. I want to feel stronger, healthier, and more like myself again.

So…here's the plan.

I'm going back to keto, paying much closer attention to water and protein this time, and changing one major variable at a time. My first peptide is GHK-Cu, the copper peptide I've been researching for hair and skin. Later, I may explore other options—but not until I have time to see what this one does for me.

My first injection was much easier than I expected. No pain. No burning. No bleeding. After hearing so many people say it burned going in, I was ready for drama. Apparently my body did not get that memo.

I'm taking photos, measurements, and notes because, well…I'm a photographer. Documentation is kind of my thing. I'll share the good, the bad, the “well, that didn't do anything,” and anything that sends me down another rabbit hole.

The part people don't see

What carrying the load left behind

My hair shedding began during a period of extreme daily stress. It wasn't one difficult week—it was prolonged stress that my body carried for a long time.

As Gen X women—and men—we often carry incredibly heavy loads alone. We care for aging parents, support our families, run businesses, manage careers, navigate grief, and keep handling whatever needs to be handled. We're the generation that learned to push through, figure it out, and avoid asking for help.

But sometimes our bodies eventually tell the story we've been too busy surviving to acknowledge.

For me, part of that story was hair shedding. Since then, I've met so many people—especially women—dealing with the same thing. The shedding may begin months after the most stressful period, which can make it feel sudden, confusing, and incredibly personal.

I usually wear a hair topper, and most people assume it's my natural hair. I'm grateful to have it because it helps me feel confident, but underneath it, I'm working to understand what happened and support my hair as it recovers.

I'm taking private baseline photos so I can document any changes accurately. I'm not ready to share those photos publicly yet—and I may never be. I'm sharing only what feels right, when it feels right.

This journey isn't simply about appearance. It's about what prolonged stress can leave behind, how often we quietly carry more than anyone realizes, and what it looks like to finally begin caring for ourselves again.

I've chosen to explore physician-guided peptide therapy as one part of my personal healthy-aging journey. I'll be documenting what I experience honestly—what changes, what doesn't, what the research says, and what I learn along the way.

If you've been carrying a heavy load too, I'd love for you to join me—not by automatically doing everything I do, but by following the journey, learning alongside me, and beginning your own conversation about healing and healthy aging.
